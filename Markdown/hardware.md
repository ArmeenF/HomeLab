# Hardware Used in Network Home Lab Version 1

## Xfinity Hybrid Fiber-Coaxial Cable Connection <img src="https://1000logos.net/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/Xfinity-logo.jpg" alt="Icon" width="40px" style="vertical-align: middle;">
- Xfinity Gigabit Internet: This is your primary internet connection, providing 1200Mbps download and 35Mbps upload speeds. It serves as the gateway for all your internet traffic.
- Download Speed: 1,200Mbps
- Upload Speed: 35Mbps

## ARRIS Surfboard S33 DOCSIS 3.1 Multi-Gigabit Cable Modem <img src="https://allvectorlogo.com/img/2016/07/arris-logo.png" alt="Icon" width="40px" style="vertical-align: middle;">
- ARRIS Surfboard S33: This is a multi-gigabit cable modem that connects directly to your Xfinity Gigabit Internet. It translates the internet signal from your ISP into a form that can be used by your internal network devices.
- A high-speed cable modem that supports DOCSIS 3.1 technology, providing multi-gigabit download speeds.

## Router Ubiquiti UDR <img src="https://static-00.iconduck.com/assets.00/ubiquiti-icon-1024x1024-v0eidwih.png" alt="Icon" width="40px" style="vertical-align: middle;">
- Router Ubiquiti UDR (UniFi Dream Router): Connected to the ARRIS Surfboard S33 via Ethernet, this router distributes the internet connection to various devices in your home network both wirelessly and through wired connections. It also provides security features like a firewall to protect your internal network from external threats.
- A router from Ubiquiti that offers advanced networking features and reliable performance for your home lab.

## NETGEAR Switch (GS305) <img src="https://logodix.com/logo/287549.png" alt="Icon" width="40px" style="vertical-align: middle;">
- NETGEAR Switch (GS305): This 5-port gigabit Ethernet unmanaged switch is connected to the UniFi Dream Router via Ethernet. It allows multiple wired devices to be connected, enabling them to communicate with each other and access the internet.
- A compact and reliable Ethernet switch from NETGEAR, offering multiple ports for connecting devices in your network.
